About Me

My name is Marc Engle, I am a former truck driver.
I worked at UPS for 5 years. I gained very valuable
experience, that I will forever cherish, during my
time over there. However, it was not something that I
could see myself doing for the rest of my life. The
schedule combined with the lack of consistency
are the two biggest reasons I chose to leave
UPS. Coding is something I have always wanted to
study, but I never went to school. Wrongfully, I
assumed coding was something you need a degree for.
Once I figured out coding bootcamps were a thing
a thing, I knew I was going to make my way into one
sooner or later. When my signifiacant other got her
new job, everything fell into place for me to have
the perfect opportunity to try something new. So,
here I am.

UPS Experience

I wanted to take this section to talk more specifically about my time at UPS. During my time at UPS I worked in their warehouse as a forklift operator with the Freight Division. After a year of that I transferred to their small package division and worked in the warehouse unloading trucks by hand for just over a year. Luckily an opportunity for me to become a truck driver opened up and I was able to make it through the 2 week training period. This was one of the toughest challenges I faced. I drove trucks for them for 3 years and it was such an amazing experience. I was able to see different parts of the Southeast region driving trucks 5 days a week. Yet, I was still able to sleep in my bed every night. That is not something most truck drivers have the luxury of. If I had to go back I would do it all over again. However, given where I want to be in the future, I knew my time there would not last forever.

UPS has taught me many skills that will be useful for my time at Coding Temple, as well as my future web development career. While there, I learned how to work well under pressure. I'm also used to working very long hours at all periods of the night or day. Given how tight some deadlines can be while coding, being used to working long hours while under pressure will be beneficial. I can't wait to see how the skills learned from UPS transfer into a software engineer's world.
